Jo Malone London Pomegranate Noir Cologne

The year 2005 was a stellar one for fragrance. It welcomed many a modern classic, from Dior Homme by Christian Dior to Alien by Mugler, Euphoria by Calvin Klein, Hypnôse by Lancôme ­– and Pomegranate Noir by Jo Malone London.

One of the renowned British lifestyle brand’s best-sellers, and – arguably, alongside Lime Basil & Mandarin – the most well-known of its colognes, Pomegranate Noir has stood the test of time, with Google searches for it recently up more than 220 per cent, and 27,000 bottles sold last December alone (that’s one every three minutes). But surprisingly, it wasn’t thought to be an instant hit for the brand, which was founded by perfumer and entrepreneur Jo Malone in 1990 and acquired by the Estée Lauder Companies in 1999.

After the cologne’s initial debut in 2005, Pomegranate Noir disappeared before re-launching within the brand’s Brit-centric collection ‘Rock the Ages’ circa 2015. It proved a perfect fit with the concept: ‘Great Britain. Proud. Illustrious. And a little eccentric. A nation renowned for its distinguished history and defining eras. Celebrated in scent by Jo Malone London.’ Representing the Victorian era within the limited-edition line-up (pictured below), it was symbolic of a golden age embracing the English drama expressed by the enigmatic depth of Pomegranate Noir. It never went away again.

“From its creation in 2005 to now, Pomegranate Noir remains a very distinctive scent with a unique olfactive,” Celine Roux, Jo Malone London’s global head of fragrance tells us. Of its enduring appeal, she offers that its versatility is key to Pomegranate Noir's success. “It has this very addictive, juicy side to it and then another sensual, warm woody side with a touch of spice.” Indeed, it’s “the combination of this contrast that makes it so unique”.

Jo Malone London Pomegranate Noir Cologne

Jo Malone London Pomegranate Noir Cologne

Created by perfumer Beverley Bayne (not, as one might assume, Malone herself – who was the nose behind other smash hits at the brand, including Lime Basil & Mandarin), Pomegranate Noir is a unisex aromatic fruity fragrance that reads as woody, warm and spicy. The top notes include pomegranate – naturally – alongside fellow red fruits rhubarb, plum, raspberry and watermelon. It’s heart features clove, pink pepper, guaiac wood, rose, jasmine and lily-of-the-valley; while at the base Virginia cedar, patchouli, amber and musk accords are all at play.

“It is amazing as a signature cologne,” (she likes to spritz Pomegranate Noir on her collar before a night out), “but also, I love it in the shower and as a body crème,” Roux says, referring to the ancillary products that support the hero perfume. “The Body Crème gives this warm sensual feel that I can accessorise with other scent,” she says.

This speaks to Jo Malone London’s long-standing philosophy of fragrance layering: the idea of pairing perfumes to make a signature scent, or to find new twists on favourites. Another reason why Pomegranate Noir sells so well is that it layers beautifully with any scent from the brand. “It brings out a sensuality,” Roux feels. “My latest favourite to combine it with is English Oak & Hazelnut,” she adds. “They are two very different woody fragrances, but the blend is just so good.” Beauty retailer Sephora recommend pairing with Lime Basil & Mandarin for a powerful impression.

Roux applies this idea of combining aroma blends beyond the body, too. “I also love Pomegranate Noir as a home scent, especially after a long day... it is so comforting," she says, recalling “I remember when I was developing the English Pear & Freesia Diffuser, I was burning a Pomegranate Noir candle by chance, and I realised how much I loved that combination. Since then, I love having these two at home”.

Should Roux be given the opportunity to update Pomegranate Noir – a practice that can be commonplace in perfume houses looking to evolve and expand on their icons – it’s not something she’d be willing to explore. “I would never touch it,” she confirms “– you can’t touch such an iconic scent”.
