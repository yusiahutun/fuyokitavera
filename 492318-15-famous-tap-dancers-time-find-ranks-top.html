15 famous tap dancers of all time: Find out who ranks top

Tap dancing has a long and rich history – it can trace its roots back to English, Irish, and African dance traditions. However, this American classic has never gone out of trend and has evolved into one of the top mainstream dance styles. But who are the famous tap dancers of all time?

Many people are drawn to tap dancing due to its unique rhythms, energy, and spunk. However, there is no denying that good tap dancers are just as interesting in the dance culture. So, who are some of the most famous tap dancers?

Top 15 most famous tap dancers

Who is known for tap dancing in the world? Below are details about the famous tap dancers in the world who have helped move this style of dancing to a new level:

1. Bill Robinson

Ever wondered who the king of tap dancing is? It's none other than Bill Robinson. Nicknamed Bojangles, he is an African-American tap dancer who is considered the best tapper of all time. Born in 1878 (died in 1949), he is best remembered for his light and exacting footwork.

Robinson had a long and prosperous tap-dancing career that spanned vaudeville, radio, television, Hollywood, and Broadway. 2. Savion Glover

Savion Glover is one of the most famous tap dancers today. He is a former tap prodigy who made his debut on Broadway at 11. He is famous for his funky dancing style, which has helped transform tap from a classical to a mainstream, contemporary dance style.

Besides dancing, Glover is also a choreographer and actor. He has choreographed and starred in several Broadway shows featuring tap dancing, including:

  • Bring in 'da Noise
  • Bring in 'da Funk
  • Shuffle Along.

3. Chloe Arnold

Chloe Arnold is undoubtedly one of the best and most famous female tap dancers in the world. The Emmy-nominated tap dancer, choreographer, director, and actress is famous for her dancing career and has appeared in several music festivals, films, and T.V. shows. Most notably, she was on FOX's dance show, So You Think You Can Dance.

Arnold also helped to launch D.C. Tap Festival. In addition, she works with an all-female crew of tap dancers whose YouTube videos are legendary.

4. John Bubbles

John Bubbles is an American tap dancer, actor, and performer. He is another one of the most famous dancers of all time. In addition, he is famous for inventing rhythm tapping, which has influenced tap dancing for several decades, thus earning him the name "father of rhythm tap." He was inducted into the Tap Dance Hall of Fame in 2002

5. Eleanor Powell

Eleanor Powell is an American dancer and actress who was one of the top entertainers during Hollywood's golden age. She is best remembered for her dazzling tap-dancing moves in several musical films:

  • Broadway Melody
  • Rosalie
  • Born to Dance.

Powell's talent for tap dancing earned her the nickname "Queen of Tap Dancing" on the silver screen. Additionally, in 1965, the Dance Masters of America declared her the World's Greatest Tap Dancer. While it has been several decades since she passed away (1982), her legacy still lives on, making her one of the greatest tap dancers of all time.

6. Steve Condos

Steve Condos is another tap dance hall of famer. He was the only Greek-American member of the Copasetics tap fraternity. He is also famous for his lighting-speed and precise, rhythmic and melodic taps, an exciting combination that always brought the audience to their feet.

Condos was also a member of The Condos Brothers, one of the top rhythm tap and wing dance crews he formed with his brothers – Frank and Nick Condos. He was also an actor who is famous for appearing in several films.

7. Fayard and Harold Nicholas

Fayard and Harold Nicholas, popularly known as The Nicholas brothers, are two of the world's most famous tap dancer brothers. They are famous for their theatrical dance performances, which blended jazz rhythm with tap dancing and acrobatics.

Fayard, the oldest of the brothers, was introduced to music from a young age before learning tap dancing at vaudeville. He then Harold the dance style before creating the "Nicholas Kids" act, which transformed into the "Nicholas Brothers" over the years. The brother's talent on the dance floor was unmatched and is their legacy. It is thus not surprising that they were inducted into the Tap Hall of Fame in 2002.

8. Anna Miller

Ann Miller, born Johnie Lucille Collier, is another famous American tap dancer and actress who made a name for herself during Hollywood's golden age. She is famous for appearing in several musicals, such as Too Darn Hot, On the Town, Kiss Me, Kate, Dames at Sea, etc., where she wowed the audience with her tap dancing.

9. Jeni LeGon

Jeni LeGon is another one of the top female tap dancers of all time. She is mostly famous for being amongst the first African-American women to pursue solo care in tap dancing. She was also a trailblazer who danced soubrette roles while wearing pants instead of short skirts.

LeGon's talent for tap led her to Broadway and Hollywood, where she even got to be the dancing partner of Bill Robinson in Hooray for Love. After retiring from acting and dancing, she set up a dance studio in L.A., where she taught tap, among other styles.

10. Charles Coles

Charles “Honi” Coles is one of the most famous male tap dancers of all time. He enjoyed a prosperous career, performing at various places such as vaudeville, concerts, and television. Many people regarded him as one of the most graceful and fasted tap dancers of his time.

On top of his solo career, Coles is also famous for his double act with fellow tapper, Charles Atkins. Later, he became an influential figure during the 1970s tap dance renaissance. He is also remembered for receiving the National Medal of Arts from President George Bush for his contributions to dance and entertainment.

11. Charles Atkins

Charles “Cholly” Atkins was a famous jazz tap dancer. He is mostly famous for being the second part of the double act Coles & Atkins, featuring tap dancer Charles Coles.

Besides dancing, Atkins is remembered for teaching tap and choreographing and stage acts for several music groups during his career. Some of his most notable students include the Moonglows, Little Anthony & The Imperials, the Cadillacs, etc.

12. Michelle Dorrance

Michelle Dorrance is an American tap dancer considered one of the top female tappers of the current times. She has enjoyed a successful career as a solo performer. In addition, she has toured with several dance groups, such as Jazz Tap Ensemble, Tap City, and STOMP.

Besides dancing, Dorrance is also a sensational choreographer and dance teacher. She previously taught dancing at the Broadway Dance Centre and has also taught at Peridance Centre and Steps on Broadway, among others.

13. Gregory Hines

Gregory Hines was an American tap dancer, singer, actor, choreographer, and dance teacher. He is famous for improvising tap steps, rhythms, and sounds. He started dancing at age 5 with his brother and had a prosperous career that spanned concerts, Hollywood, and even Broadway.

Besides dancing, Hines taught tap to many students, including Savio Glover. His contribution to tap dancing and entertainment has won him many accolades, including Tony, Drama Desk, and Daytime Emmy awards.

14. Michael Flatley

Ever wondered who is the fastest tap dancer? It's no other than Michael Flatley. He is an Irish-American dancer famous for dance shows such as:

  • Feet of Flames
  • Lord of the Dance
  • Riverdance
  • Celtic Tiger Live

Flatley mostly focuses on traditional Irish dance styles. However, he also incorporates moves from other dancing genres, including rhythm and tap. He once held the record for the fastest tap dancing in the world, with 35 taps per second.

15. Jumaane Taylor

Jumaane Taylor is an American tap dancer hailing from Chicago. He got into tap dancing at seven and made his professional debut with the dance company M.A.D.D. Rhythms. His career as a tapper has seen him perform at top places, including the Jazz Institute of Chicago and the Museum of Contemporary Art.

Besides dancing, Taylor is also a choreographer and director. He is the creator of Supreme Love, a tap dance production featured at various music and dance festivals. His exemplary work saw him receive the 2019 3Arts Award.
