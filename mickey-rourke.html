Biography Timeline

Mickey Rourke is an American actor, screenwriter, and former boxer who rose to fame in the 1980s with his work in films such as “Diner”, “Rumble Fish”, and “The Pope of Greenwich Village”. Rourke’s career experienced a lull in the 1990s, but he made a comeback in the 2000s with his performance in the film “The Wrestler”, which earned him a Golden Globe and an Academy Award nomination. This is a detailed biography timeline of Mickey Rourke.

September 16, 1952: Mickey Rourke was born in Schenectady, New York.

1971-1973: Rourke attends the Miami Beach Police Department Reserve Academy and becomes a police officer quickly.

1979: Rourke makes his film debut in “1941” directed by Steven Spielberg.

1980: Rourke stars in “Fade to Black” and appears in “Heaven’s Gate”.

1981: Rourke gains attention for his role in the film “Body Heat”.

1982: Rourke stars in the films “Diner” and “Rumble Fish”.

1983: Rourke stars in “The Pope of Greenwich Village”.

1985: Rourke stars in the film “Year of the Dragon”.

1986: Rourke stars in the film “9½ Weeks”.

1991-1995: Rourke takes a hiatus from acting and pursues a career in professional boxing.

1997: Rourke returns to acting with a role in “Double Team” alongside Jean-Claude Van Damme.

2005: Rourke appears in the film “Sin City” directed by Robert Rodriguez.

2008: Rourke stars in the film “The Wrestler”, directed by Darren Aronofsky, which earns him a Golden Globe and an Academy Award nomination for Best Actor.

2009: Rourke wins the Independent Spirit Award for Best Male Lead for his role in “The Wrestler”.

2010: Rourke stars in the film “Iron Man 2” as the villain Ivan Vanko.

2019: Rourke stars in the film “Night Walk”, directed by Aziz Tazi.
