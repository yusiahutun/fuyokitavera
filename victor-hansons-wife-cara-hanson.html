Hanson’s Wife Is a Helpful Person

His work as an author and a University professor keeps the Stanford graduate busy. His wife, on the other hand, keeps herself busy with a variety of philanthropic endeavors. She updates her Facebook profile with information about her contributions.

For example, on May 12, 2019, she made a significant contribution to Angela N Dustin Freeman’s birthday fundraiser for the Smith-Magenis Syndrome Research Foundation. On May 17, 2020, she contributed to Krista Busch Lara’s birthday fundraiser to benefit the Lucile Packard Foundation for Children’s Health. Such incidents reveal her compassionate nature, and she is frequently involved in charitable works.

Mr. and Mrs. Hanson Experienced a Tragedy

The author of ‘The Second World Wars’ and his wife had three children. However, on November 13, 2014, they lost their youngest daughter, Susannah Merry, to an unknown illness. Susannah’s death shook the Hanson family, and, understandably, the family struggled to move on from the tragedy.

Susannah’s family expressed their love for her in an emotional obituary. Her passion for life and adventure, her willingness to help everyone, and her love for her family were all mentioned in it, along with other heartbreaking statements. With time, the family came to terms with the loss and resumed some semblance of normalcy. Pauline Hanson, Hanson’s eldest daughter, and William Hanson, Hanson’s son, carry on the Hanson family legacy.

A Brief Overview of the Professor’s Career

Before all of the author’s success and fame, he was an orchard and vineyard grower who lived in the house his grandparents built on their farm. Hanson, on the other hand, joined the CSU Fresno campus in 1984 and established a classical languages program.

Seven years later, in 1991, his teaching was recognized with an ‘American Philological Association Excellence Award,’ which is given annually to the country’s top undergraduate Greek and Latin teachers.

Stanford alumni worked as National Endowment for the Humanities fellows at the Center for Advanced Studies in the Behavioral Sciences from 1992 to 1993. Following that, he began writing for various news outlets such as the New York Times, National Review, International Herald Tribune, Los Angeles Times, Wall Street Journal, and New York Post.

He began writing a weekly column for PJ Media in 2006 and continued until 2016. Hanson began writing a weekly column for American Greatness in 2017 and continues to do so. He has also written and edited 24 books.
