Paz created a group called Fuerza Regida together with Khrystain Ramos and Sammy Jaimez at a barbershop. Initially, Jesus was to play bass, but he became the lead singer after the original lead singer left the band. The band performed covers in local underground gigs, also known as flyer parties.

They gradually built a considerable following, mainly because of how they reinvented traditional Mexican corridos with hip-hop. Finally, in 2018, they went mainstream when their hit song Radicamos En South Central, a tribute to their Socal roots, went viral. As a result, they got the attention of music executives and brands, including Rancho Humidle, which signed them.

A record label made it easier for Jesus and his group to write and produce songs. Furthermore, they started selling out shows in New York, Texas, and America. Soon enough, people began noticing the group because of their flair for unique urban songs combined with traditional Mexican elements.
