Who is Pat Fitzgerald?

Pat Fitzgerald, whose real name is Patrick William Fitzgerald Jr, was born on 2 December 1974 in Moline, Illinois, United States, meaning he is 48 years old as of 2023. His parents are Pat Fitzgerald Sr (father) and Flo Fitzgerald (mother).

Pat Fitzgerald's career

Pat had a passion for football at a young age and started his career in 1993 with college football. He played as a linebacker at Northwestern University for four years, from 1993 to 1996.

While at the university, the renowned star was the first two-time winner of the Bronko Nagurski and Chuck Bednarik Awards, given to the best defensive player in college football.

In 1996 Fitzgerald added the Jack Lambert Trophy as best linebacker to his awards. He also bagged the Big Ten Medal of Honor in 1997 for his athletic and academic excellence.

The former football player worked under coach Ron Vanderlinden of the University of Maryland shortly after graduating from college. Additionally, he worked under Coach Gary Barnett and Randy Walker as an assistant coach before becoming the head coach.
