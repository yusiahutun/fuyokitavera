MAFS Australia Bronte's 'wardrobe malfunction' distracts viewers from kiss scandal

Married at First Sight Australia viewers were left distracted as Bronte Schofield's dress appeared to "come undone".

The brunette bombshell sizzled in a blue gown as she attended the wedding of two new couples who joined the E4 experiment during Tuesday's (March 28) episode.

She looked gorgeous as ever as she showed off her petite figure in the barely-there gown as she joined husband Harrison Boon to welcome the newlyweds.

Drama kicked off in during the ceremony as Claire Nomarhas told husband Jesse Burford about her secret kiss with their co-star Adam Seed, who is married to Janelle Han.

However, fans were left distracted during Wednesday's (March 29) edition of the E4 series as they were convinced Bronte's dress had "come undone" as she gave a heartbroken Jesse a hug.

Taking to Twitter, one fan penned: "Off topic, but How does Brontë not know that her dress is undone?"

Another viewer replied: "Ha ha I thought it was just backless but I was wondering how it stayed in place just did a rewind."

A third person posted: "Did Bronte’s dress come undone?"

While a fourth social media user: "Emm is Bronte's dress falling off her or meant to be the design?!"

Opening up about the cheating scandal, Claire told Jesse: "I have broken your trust.

"That night when we all went out and Adam and I went outside we kissed."

Jesse went onto say he "knew it" and called Adam a "snake".

Claire and Jesse went on a night out with some of the other cast members including Adam.

Claire and Adam were said to be getting on well, and when Claire returned home later that evening after Jesse, she was said to be on the phone with someone, who Jesse claimed was Adam.

Both Claire and Adam denied anything happened between them, with Jesse later apologising to both his partner and the fellow couple for flying off the handle, admitting to having a jealous side.

However, Clarie's confession proved that Jesse was right to be concerned about Adam and Claire's closeness.
