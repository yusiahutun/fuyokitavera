Why to Go to the Hamptons in the Winter-Spend Winter in the Hamptons

1. Sip cider in a historic mansion. Cabin fever may be a thing, but mansion fever? Impossible. You'd be hard pressed to get antsy during a stay at Topping Rose House , a historic-home-turned-boutique hotel in Bridgehampton. The rooms are cozy, the minibars are stocked with local treats (they're complimentary, by the way) and the fire is blazing in the restaurant downstairs. Should you feel like stepping out, slip into a pair of Hunter wellies (you'll find a couple pairs in your room) and borrow one of the hotel's Lexus SUVs to go for a spin.

Courtesy Topping House

2. See a vineyard decked in Christmas lights. In a move that puts most tree-decorating traditions to shame, Wölffer Estate Vineyard in Sagaponack wraps their vines in some 15,000 LED lights starting in December. Take in the spectacle and then sip cabernet sauvignon and nibble on a cheese platter in the winery's tasting room.

Courtesy Wölffer Estate Vineyard

3. Visit an oyster farm. As the saying goes, you should only eat oysters in months that contain the letter "R" in the name (December, January, February, etc.). So what better time to stop by Widow's Hole Oyster Co. in Greenport than in prime feasting season? Owner Mike Osinski, who often sports an "Eat Live Meat" tee, will give you a tour. He and his wife have hosted some of New York City's top chefs at their historic home on the property. The hospitable pair may even invite you to sit around a bonfire after you've sampled the freshly shucked goods.

Courtesy Widow's Hole Oyster Co.

4. Check out world-class art without the crowds. The buzz this month may have been all about Art Basel in Miami Beach, but the Parrish Art Museum in sleepy Water Mill packs an artistic punch, too.As you take in portraits by Cindy Sherman and Annie Leibovitz, as well as paintings by Warren Brandt and Raymond Parker, there's a good chance you'll have entire galleries to yourself. The Parrish also has a café and a shop stocked with stationery and design books.

Courtesy Parrish Art Museum

5. Skate on an NHL-sized rink. Summer in the Hamptons means boogie boarding and surfing, while winter in the Hamptons means toe picks and triple Axels (that is, of course, if you can swing it). The Buckskill Winter Club in East Hampton has an open-air ice-skating rink and hosts regular public skating nights on the weekends. There's also a warming house with a fire and hot chocolate because, as we all know, that's half the fun.

Courtesy

6. Get Your Salt Water Fix Indoors. The Hamptons is best known for sun, sand and salty water, and while we can't promise 90 degree days in January, Gurney's Montauk offers the Seawater Spa, complete with a heated, sand-filtered, ocean-fed indoor seawater pool . Look out onto the real ocean post-dip (and possibly post-massage) to complete the experience.
