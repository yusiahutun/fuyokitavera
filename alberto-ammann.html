Alberto Ammann is an Argentine actor who has played various roles in several movies, television, and theater. Alberto Ammann is broadly known for Pacho Herrera in Netflix’s Narcos.

Early Life

Alberto Ammann was born in Argentina on October 20, 1978. According to his birth date, he is 45 years old under the zodiac sign Libra. Talking about his nationality, he is an Argentinian.

Regarding his family background, he is the son of  Nelida Rey (Mother) and Luis Alberto Ammann (Father). His father, Luis is a well-known journalist, writer, and politician.

Speaking about his siblings, there is no information available in the media. It seems that he is the only child of his parents.

Moreover, His family fled Argentina to take shelter in Spain to evade the claws of military rule that was gripping the country. He was only a year old when they fled and they didn’t return until he was four years old.

Career

Regarding his career, he made his film debut in 2009 in the movie ‘Cell 21’ in which he appeared as Juan Oliver, a prison guard who gets mistaken for a prisoner when the prison gets taken over by an inmate named Malamadre.

Moreover, he won the Best New Actor Award for this role at the XXIV Goya Awards on 14 February 2010. In 2010, Ammann appeared as the Spanish Playwright and Port Lope de Vega in the movie ‘Lope’.

In 2015, he appeared as gay Cali Cartel member Helmer Herrera in the Netflix series, Narcos. Likewise, he appeared as Javier Delgado, hydrologist and geochemist, in National Geographic’s mini-series ‘Mars’ in 2016.
