Robert Allan Caro is an American journalist and author known for biographies of American politicians Robert Moses and Lyndon B. Johnson. After years working as a journalist, Caro wrote The Power Broker, a biography of New York City planner Robert Moses, selected by the Modern Library as one of the hundred best. about non-fiction books of the twentieth century. Since then, he has written four of the five tentative volumes of Lyndon Johnson's Years, a biography of the former president. For his biography, he has won two Pulitzer prizes for biography, two National Book awards, the Francis Parkman Prize, three National Book Critics' Awards, and the Mencken Prize for Good Book. First, the Carr P. Collins award from the Texas Library, the DB Hardeman Prize and the Gold Medal of Biography of the American Academy of Arts and Literature. In 2010, President Barack Obama awarded Caro the National Medal for the Humanities.
