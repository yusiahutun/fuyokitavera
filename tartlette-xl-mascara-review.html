My thoughts on mascara have changed a lot since my first review a few years ago. So many new formulas have come out, and of course I’ve tried them all. One stand out mascara “type” — yes there are types! — that I can’t get enough of is tubing mascara. If you’re unfamiliar, tubing mascaras create actual tube extensions on your individual lashes, meaning they appear longer, more fanned out, and more gorgeous than ever. To remove the tubes at the end of the day, all you have to do is slide them off your lashes with warm water.

And although this removal process is much easier than scrubbing off waterproof formulas, the best part of tubing mascara is that most formulas are usually water-resistant, like this Tartlette XL mascara that’s exclusive to QVC. So in addition to tubing mascara being automatically lengthening, since it’s water-resistant, it won’t smudge or flake off over time, which is usually my biggest mascara conundrum. This formula is way less flakier than the original Tartlette formula. Both of them definitely have minimal flakes, but I’m always looking for completely flake-free formulas, and this Tartlette XL formula is as close as I’ve gotten with any Tarte mascara formula.

I mean, seriously, just look at my lashes after applying!! As I was reading the reviews on the QVC page, I noticed a lot of reviewers saying the mascara came out way too thick or clumpy upon first application. This is a mascara issue that I’ve thought A LOT ABOUT. Usually, when you break open a new tube, there is tons of formula packed onto the brush that’s gathered there during the shipping process.

I find that it’s best to keep your OLD mascara wand nearby and once you apply the new formula to lashes, get your older wand and help remove some of the product/fan out the product even further. After a few weeks of use, your new mascara wand will adjust and you can put on a more even application. Yes, it’s annoying, but it literally happens with ALL mascaras, so the next time you deal with this, remember my helpful hint.
