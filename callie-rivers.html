Career

Callie Rivers was born into a sport-loving family. Her father is a former NBA player and current basketball coach for many reputed teams. She, naturally, gravitated towards sports at a young age.

While studying at Winter Park High School in Florida, she started playing volleyball for her high school team. She also delivered conclusive victories for her school.

Therefore, it was under her that the school won 6A State titles in as little as three seasons. Apart from these achievements, she also set career records at the school level.

She had to her name 1394 kills, 2356 attempts, and 184.5 blocks. She was also ranked second in the list for digs and aces and was ranked third in hitting percentage of all time.

Moreover, she ended her school year with a high as she was named the ‘Florida Gatorade High School Player of the Year’.

While at school, Rivers actively represented her city and state at different levels. Therefore, she played for the USA Junior National A2 Team during the USA Junior Olympic Volleyball tournament.

Similarly, in 2006, she was named the most valuable player in the USA High-Performance Championships. Moreover, these accolades helped her flag off a sports career while in college.

More about her career

She enrolled at the University of Florida in 2007 and continued playing volleyball. In the same year, she recorded the fourth-highest kills per game average (2.71) and the fifth-highest points per game average (3.29) in the university history by a freshman.

Furthermore, in 2008, she played a decisive role in the team’s victorious SEC title. Likewise, for her contributions during the season, she was named to the AVCA All-America honorable mentions.

And, in her third year, she succeeded in joining the AVCA All-America Third Team. In her final year at University, she was named to the AVCA All-America First Team, an honor that she prided upon.

Likewise, after graduating, she signed a contract with Leonas de Ponce, her first professional club, during the Liga de Voleibol Superior Femenino. But, she only played for a year before deciding to retire.

Even after retiring from her sports career, she continued to be eagerly noticed by the media.
