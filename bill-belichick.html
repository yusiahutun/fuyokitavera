Bill Belichick - Bio, Age, In Relation, Net Worth, Facts, Nationality

Table of Biography

Bill Belichick is a professional football coach from the United States. Bill Belichick is currently the head coach of the New England Patriots of the NFL.

Early Life

Bill Belichick was born William Stephen Belichick on 16th April 1952. He stepped into this world in Nashville, Tennessee, United States. By this, he is 71 years old as of 2022 and American by nationality. Similarly, he follows Christianity and was born under the birth sign of Aries.

Moving on to his family members, he was born to his father Stephen Nickolas Belichick, and his mother Jeannette. His father was an American football player and a coach. Likewise, his paternal grandparents are Ivan Biličić and Marija. Similarly, he spent his childhood in Annapolis, Maryland.

Education

Talking about his educational status, he did his schooling at Annapolis High School in 1970. He played football and lacrosse at his school. After this, he joined Phillips Academy in Massachusetts for a postgraduate year. In 2011, the school honored him by inducting him into its Athletics Hall of Honor, 40 years later. In addition, the football coach also attended Wesleyan University where he received his bachelor’s degree in economics in 1975.

Professional Life and Career

Following graduation, Belichick began working as an assistant to Baltimore Colts head coach Bill Belichick. He became an assistant special teams coach for the Detroit Lions in 1976.

In 1979, Belichick began a 12-year run as a defensive assistant and special teams coach with the New York Giants, working alongside head coach Ray Perkins. In 1980, he added linebackers coaching to his responsibilities, and in 1985, he was named defensive coordinator under Bill Parcells, who had replaced Perkins in 1983. Following the 1986 and 1990 seasons, the Giants won Super Bowls XXI and XXV.

Furthermore, he worked as the head coach of the Cleveland Browns from 1991 to 1995. After being fired by the Cleveland Browns, he returned to the Patriots as an assistant head coach and defensive backs coach for the 1996 season.

Belichick was named interim head coach of the Jets in February 1997. The Patriots finished the regular season 5–11 and missed the playoffs in 2000. They won the Super Bowl in 2001 after going 11–5 in the regular season and defeating the Oakland Raiders and Pittsburgh Steelers. Likewise, the Patriots went 9–7 the following season, their first match in Gillette Stadium, and missed the playoffs.

More Details

The Patriots concluded with a 14–2 record in 2004 and advanced to the Divisional Round by defeating the Indianapolis Colts. The Patriots ended the 2006 season with a 12–4 record and a 37–16 win over the New York Jets in the Wild Card Round.

Belichick led the Patriots to their first-ever flawless regular season in 2007. In the first quarter of the Patriots’ 2008 season opener against the Kansas City Chiefs, quarterback Tom Brady suffered a season-ending injury. Matt Cassel, the backup quarterback, has been designated the starter for the rest of the season. Belichick led the Patriots to another AFC East division win with a 10–6 record in 2009, with a completely healthy Tom Brady as the starting quarterback.

Belichick and the Patriots had a 14–2 record in 2010, a 13–3 record in 2011, a 2-2 record in 2012, 12-4 in 2015, 14-2 in 2016, and 13-3 in 2017. On May 13, 2019, Belichick revealed that he would be the new head coach of the New England Patriots.
