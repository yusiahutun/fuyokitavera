Aly Kaneshiro Bio, Age, Height, Boyfriend, Parents, Wiki

Aly Kaneshiro of Standford Cardinal has captured the attention of fans and analysts for her performance in NCAA. With an impressive debut season as a freshman and a sophomore campaign marked by noteworthy achievements, Kaneshiro has emerged as a vital asset to the Stanford softball team. 

Early Life

A native of Santa Clarita, California, Aly Kaneshiro is the daughter of Lance and Kathy Caneshiro. Kaneshiro’s passion for sports runs deep within her family. With two accomplished brothers, Colby and Ryan, who played baseball and basketball at the collegiate level, respectively, athletics is an integral part of their lives. Additionally, Kaneshiro’s cousin, Keston Hiura, made a name for himself as a baseball player at UC Irvine and currently plays for the Milwaukee Brewers.

Aly Kaneshiro showcased her talents in high school softball at William S. Hart High School in Newhall, California. A two-time team captain and a consistent performer, she earned accolades such as All-Foothill League and All-Santa Clarita honors. Kaneshiro’s excellence extended beyond the softball field, as she also excelled in basketball, garnering similar recognition.

Aly Kaneshiro Career

Freshman 

Kaneshiro’s impact was apparent from her debut season as a freshman. Making 30 starts, primarily as a designated player, she proved to be a valuable asset to the team’s offensive firepower. Batting .265/.314/.459 in 98 at-bats, she scored 10 runs and drove in 15 runs, showcasing her ability to contribute in crucial moments.

Highlighting her freshman year was a remarkable performance against No. 13/14 Arizona State, where Kaneshiro became the first Stanford freshman since 2010 to hit two home runs in a game, amassing six RBIs in a standout performance. She also hit her first career home run against Arizona, adding to her impressive offensive contributions. In the NCAA Tournament, Kaneshiro continued to shine, homering against South Dakota State.

Sophomore

During her sophomore season, Kaneshiro showcased her versatility and impact on the field, appearing in 61 games while starting 60, primarily as a catcher. She also took on the role of designated player when needed, demonstrating her ability to contribute in various positions.

Bolstering the Cardinal’s offensive power, Kaneshiro achieved a batting average of .240, contributing five doubles and four impressive home runs, tallying a total of 20 runs batted in. Her powerful swing was on full display when she homered against BYU, leaving a lasting impact on the game. Kaneshiro also recorded a memorable home run in a victory over San Jose State, underlining her ability to rise to the occasion in crucial matchups.

One standout performance for Kaneshiro came against Mount St. Mary’s, where she drove in three runs, scored twice, and showcased her home run prowess. Additionally, on the same day, she played an instrumental role in catching Molly Millar’s historic no-hitter, further solidifying her defensive excellence behind the plate.
