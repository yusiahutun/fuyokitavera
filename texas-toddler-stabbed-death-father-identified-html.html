Texas toddler stabbed to death 'by his father' identified

Officials in Texas on Monday identified the 16-month-old boy who police say was stabbed to death by his deranged father as Ashton Ness. 

The Tarrant County Medical Examiner's revealed the victim's name, but not his cause of death. 

Police in Lewisville have not identified Ashton's father, who was hospitalized after being shot in the leg by a neighbor in a desperate bid to stop the deadly knife attack on his toddler son on Sunday afternoon.

But based on information and photos posted on Facebook pages that appear to belong to Ashton's parents, the boy's father is believed to be 28-year-old Blair Ness.  

The dad was filmed being dragged away by police after the stabbing frenzy, during which he was heard screaming, 'Jesus is coming.'

Lewisville Police Capt Mike Lane told DallasNews.com they were withholding the suspect's name pending his transfer to jail because the hospital where his is being treated is 'not a real secure facility.' 

According to an update sent to DailyMail.com on Tuesday, the suspect has been arraigned in the hospital on a charge of capital murder, and his bond has been set at $1.5million. 

A social media account belonging to a Blair Ness reveals that just five days before the horrific stabbing, the father-of-one posted a status update gushing about his family and his Christian faith, which was accompanied by a photo of his girlfriend and their son, Ashton. 

'The start of something beautiful! I love my family, man do we have a strong bond. let's all stand strong in jesus christ cause it's so much better than magic. love you guys so much,' he wrote.  

Lewisville Police Capt Jesse Hunter said that based on witness accounts, the father carried Ashton out of an apartment at the Oak Forrest Apartments in the 1500 block of Texas 121 Business in Lewisville shortly before 1pm on Sunday. 

Neighbors James Ellis and Jolyn Zapatos told CBS DFW that the man slammed his son to the concrete, then knelt beside him, took out a small knife and proceeded to stab him in the abdomen.
