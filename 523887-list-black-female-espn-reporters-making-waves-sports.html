List of black female ESPN reporters making waves in sports

Working for ESPN is a dream come true for many sportscasters. Gone are the days when sports broadcasting was a male-dominated industry. Today, women are getting as good as men at analysis, sports coverage hosting, writing, and all-around reporting. Think of your favourite sports reporters for channels like ESPN. You will undoubtedly have a handful of black female ESPN reporters on your list.

Sports leagues in the United States have massive followings. From the NBA to soccer, you will find dedicated fans who enjoy watching reporters as much as they love the players.

The industry's women reporters are promising and boost the viewership by bringing a fresh perspective. Black women are largely learning the ropes and filling media studios as they showcase their mastery and prowess in sports.

10 black female ESPN reporters

A good bunch of black female ESPN reporters have had a background in sports, either as players, coaches, or simply being sports enthusiasts from when they were young. Here is a list of the best black ESPN female reporters according to Black Covery:

1. Lisa Salters

Lisa Salters is one of the famous female ESPN reporters who has been working for ESPN on ABC since 2000. Having been in sports journalism for over three decades, Lisa has covered some high-profile sports events like NBA Sunday Showcase and Saturday Primetime Matches.

She has also worked in the Middle East. The former college basketball player previously covered the O. J. Simpson murder case for ABC and was hired as a reporter at WBAL-TV in Baltimore between 1988 and 1995.

2. Kara Lawson

Kara Lawson wears many hats. She is the head coach of the Duke Blue Devils women's basketball team and a basketball television analyst for ESPN and the Washington Wizards. The 48-year-old was an athlete herself before joining TV.

She is a former WNBA player, where she played as a shooting guard. As a player, she played for the Sacramento Monarchs, Connecticut Sun, and Washington Mystics between 2003 and 2015. As a coach, Kara has worked for the Boston Celtics as an assistant and Duke, where she currently is.

3. Malika Andrews

Malika Andrews joined ESPN in 2018 as an online NBA writer. The sports journalist and reporter currently hosts the NBA Today, which replaced The Jump. Since she joined ESPN, Malika Andrews has received immense recognition for her work.

She was the youngest sideline reporter for a broadcast during the 2020 NBA Bubble and was named one of the Forbes 30 Under 30 in the sports industry for 2021.

4. LaChina Robinson

LaChina Robinson is a basketball analyst who calls college basketball games for ESPN, Fox Sports 1, and FS South. She is one of the tallest ESPN black reporters, standing 6 feet and 4 inches tall.

The award-winning host and analyst has bagged multiple accolades throughout her career. They include the 2009 Atlanta Dream Women of Inspiration Award, the 2018 Dawn Staley Excellence in Broadcasting Award, and the 2021 Mel Greenberg Media Award.

5. Elle Duncan

Elle started her career in sports journalism when she was hired as an intern with the 2 Live Stews. She has since worked for the radio station V-103 as a contributor and reporter for the Atlanta Falcons, Atlanta Hawks, and New England Sports Network, among other media entities.

The 40-year-old sports anchor and TV personality joined ESPN in 2016, where she hosts the weekday 6 pm ET edition of SportsCenter with Kevin Negandhi. She also appeared as a news reporter in the movie Ride Along (2014).

6. Christy Winters Scott

Christy Winter Scott is no average sports journalist, as her career spans over three decades. Her star started shining early when she played high school basketball and was named one of the greatest female players to play at South Lakes.

Christy Winters Scott has also worked as a coach before getting to broadcasting. She presently works as a basketball colour analyst for college basketball games for ESPN, FSN, The Big Ten Network, NBC Sports Washington, and Raycom Sports.

7. Ros Gold-Onwude

The Stanford University alumna has been interested in sports since she was in school. While at Staford, Ros Gold-Onwude was part of the women's basketball team, where she played in three Final Fours and two national championship games.

Her journey in sports journalism started at Pac-12 Networks, where she was a basketball analyst. She joined ESPN as an analyst in 2010, worked until 2017, and rejoined the sports channel in 2020. The 36-year-old is one of the black women on ESPN First Take.

8. Kimberly A. Martin

Kimberly A. Martin joined ESPN as an NFL reporter in March 2020. The Brooklyn native has had a colourful career, having previously worked at Yahoo! Sports and The Washington Post’s sports department.

The veteran sports journalist is the black woman on ESPN Get Up. She also hosts programs like Sunday NFL Countdown, Fantasy Football Now, NFL Live, Around The Horn, and SportsCenter. You can also catch her on ESPN's only all-female podcast, First Take Her Take.

Some awards Martin has bagged in her career include the National Association of Black Journalists, Emerging Journalist of the Year Award in 2011 and the Associated Press Sports Editors' Projecting Writing Award in 2015.

9. Kendra Andrews

She is not just the younger sister to Malika Andrews but a sports enthusiast, budding journalist, and talented reporter. The 26-year-old is an Alma mater of Gonzaga University and grew up as a fan of the Golden State Warriors.

Before joining ESPN, Kendra Andrews worked for NBC Sports Bay Area and The Athletic. She joined ESPN in 2022 and works as a writer and contributor covering the Golden State Warriors.

10. Terrika Foster-Brasby

The HBCU alumna describes herself as self-driven, self-motivated, and selfless. Terrika Foster-Brasby works as a reporter, analyst, and host for ESPN.

Before joining the sports channel, Foster-Brasby worked for Yahoo, Contributor Network, and appeared on other media companies like Fox Sports Radio, NPR, CBS Sports Network, and Amazon Prime Sports.
