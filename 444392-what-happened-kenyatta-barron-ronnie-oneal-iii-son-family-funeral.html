What happened to Kenyatta Barron?

On March 18, 2018, Kenyatta Barron, 33-years old, and her daughter, Ron’Niveya, were murdered. On top of this, Barron's 8-years old son was wounded during the incident but survived the attack. So, what went down that caused these horrible murders?

The person to blame for Kenyatta Barron’s son's injury and murder is Ronnie Oneal III. According to investigators handling the case, Ronnie shot and beat her girlfriend, Kenyatta Barron, with a shotgun.

After this, he proceeded to Kenyatta's daughter, hitting her with a hatchet. He later wound Kenyatta Barron's son, Ronni Jr. and set the house on fire.

Ronnie Jr. escaped the home with stab wounds and burns and informed detectives that his father had shot his mother before being transported to a hospital for treatment.

When police arrived at the scene at 11:59 pm, Kenyatta Barron's daughter died. Ronnie Oneal III was arrested after discovering the incident, though he first resisted and had to be tasered by police officers.

While inside the police vehicle, he called Kenyatta Barron the devil and mentioned that the children were the devil's kids. Ronnie was booked into Hillsborough County Jail the following day.

Kenyatta’s son survived to tell a shocking tale

Four days after the incident, on March 22, 2018, Ronnie Jr. recounted the events of the murders to detectives, and they were shocking. According to his story, he figured out how to get out of the house where a neighbour found him.

However, Ronnie Jr. altered his statement while in court when his dad, who represented himself in the case, was interviewing him. He stated that he did not see his father murdering anybody during the testimony. He likely made the statement out of the fear of his father, who appeared to be aggressive.

The trial for the Kenyatta Barron and daughter murders

Kenyatta Barron's family was put on the spot during the trial as the prosecutors tried to unravel the story and give justice to the aggrieved. The trial started on June 16, 2021, and the survivor, Ronnie Jr., was one of the witnesses.
