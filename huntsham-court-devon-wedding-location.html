Huntsham Court In Devon Is The Perfect Country Wedding Venue

Nestled in the quiet, sleepy village of Tiverton in Devon, Huntsham Court is a blissfully uncrowded Grade II listed countryside property with sustainability at its heart.

Damain Llambias and Christopher Badham renovated the 104-room Victorian property back in 2011 after searching for a slow-paced home away from the bustle of London. They wanted somewhere people could rent out for weddings, events and parties that felt far removed from hotel chains in the city. "We bought the house to restore and sustain it, while giving guests a chance to enjoy it. We thought that if we collect together the great and the good of the South West, it would bring revenue into the area," Christopher tells Country Living.

This place has an easy-going loveliness about it. Renowned architect William Burge, who was asked to update the property, preserved the building’s heritage, keeping the original features but pepping it up with modern furniture and state-of-the-art electrics.

Huntsham Court

The interiors are what sets this place apart. Bedrooms are done up with grand four-poster beds, cheerful splashes of colour, vibrant wallpaper (one room even has the original wallpaper that’s now over 100-years-old), wooden beams, sultry bathrooms and bottles of aged whiskey for guests to enjoy as a post-dinner treat.

Perhaps the best room of all in the house is the library. Rows of jacket-less books (most of which are French) line the large four walls. It’s Damain’s favourite room in the house, a place where he explained he could easily spend hours. Large cushiony-soft velvet sofas create a cosy space for wedding guests to relax, while floor-to-ceiling windows provide extravagant views.

Matt Austin

Pet owners will love that this venue is incredibly dog-friendly. Doggy treats are made by the chef so your pup won't feel left out when the three-course dinner is served, and the large surrounding grounds provide a brilliant place for games of fetch.

Wedding guests have the chance to really make this house their own. A tiny kitchen area has everything for people to take as they please: a kettle, Nespresso coffee machine, packets of biscuits, fruit, and nuts. And if that wasn't enough, the chefs working in the larger kitchen next door will whip up anything you want, whenever you want it.

Lee Bochicchio

"The chef is always going to local farms to find the best local produce in the area"

The food served has sustainability at its core, serving up simple and delicious local produce. Guests can expect a proper farm-to-fork experience, as chef Peter Mundy delivers a down-to-earth service people won’t forget. "Peter who heads up the food here is always going to local farms to find the best local produce in the area," explains Christopher.

"A lot of people who stay here want to have a conscientious wedding or party where their ethical footprint is as small as it can be. As much as it being about the wellbeing of the animals, it’s also about having a smaller footprint, so we really try to support local farms."

Huntsham Court

On-site country pursuits include archery, clay pigeon shooting, country walks and spa indulgences. Shady gardens provide a lovely break from the wedding crowds, while unprecedented views offer the best way to spend an afternoon.
