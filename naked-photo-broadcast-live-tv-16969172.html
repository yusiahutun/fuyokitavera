Shock as naked photo is broadcast on live TV after viewers send in pics

People watching the live television programme in Argentina got quite the eyeful when a picture of a naked man was broadcast on screen.

The photo had been accidentally included alongside others sent in by viewers as part of a festive review.

The clip shows the programme’s hosts clearly left red-faced by the explicit blunder.

Presenters had asked viewers to send in their photos as part of a segment on Three Kings Day – a holiday also known as Epiphany – which is celebrated on January 6 in South America.

Three Kings Day is a festive celebration of the Three Wise Men coming to visit Jesus for the first time.

Good Day Argentina had requested photos of children taking part in a tradition that sees them leaving out shoes and hay – win the same way many in the UK leave mince pies and carrots for Santa and his reindeer.

But as photos of the excited children were shown on screen as part of a slideshow it suddenly cut to a picture of a fully nude man.

After the explicit mishap, one of the presenters covers his face and laughs uncomfortably before trying to carry on with the show.

The blunder was never mentioned on air but the popular daytime telly show didn’t manage to get away with it entirely – shocked viewers at home posted the embarrassing gaffe on social media quickly sending it viral.

It’s not the first time people have been unexpectedly treated to accidentally nudity.
