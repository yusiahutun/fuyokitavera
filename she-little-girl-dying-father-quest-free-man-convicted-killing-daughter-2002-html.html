Father is trying to free Jamerson Mangrum convicted of killing his daughter

A father dying of cancer is convinced that the Georgia man who was convicted of killing his teenage daughter is innocent.

Joe Hamlin is working desperately to free, Jamerson Mangrum, who was convicted of killing his daughter, Katie, in July of 2002 in Acworth.

'She was my little girl. I miss her a lot,' Joe Hamlin told WGCL.

The 15-year-old's body was found nude and partially burned along Kemp Road near Kellogg Creek. 

Tragic: Katie Hamlin (photo above) was murdered in July of 2002 in Acworth, Georgia. Her body was found nude and partially burned 

Heartbreaking: Joe Hamlin, Katie's father, says that he misses his daughter a lot and that she was 'his little angel'

In the weeks following her murder, authorities interviewed a number of her acquaintances, including then 17-year-old Jamerson Mangrum who denied that he'd seen her.   

However, when an autopsy found Mangrum's DNA on her body, he changed his story, saying the pair had consensual sex.   

He was found guilty of murder, rape and a number of other crimes in 2005 and was sentenced to life in prison with an additional 80 years.

Convicted: A jury found Jamerson Mangrum (photo above) guilty of Katie Hamlin's murder in 2005. He was sentenced to life in prison with an additional 80 years

Hamlin learned new information about his daughter's murder a few months ago and he now believes that Mangrum is innocent.

Mangrum's mother gave Hamlin all the 'discovery material' in the case and after reviewing it, he wrote a letter seeking answers to the incarcerated man, WGCL reported.

'I sent him a list of about 19 questions and built in some control questions that I knew the answers to,' Hamlin said. 

'He answered every one of them honestly'

'Jamerson was at home. This is verified by phone records which I have copies of.' 

Dedicated: Joe Hamlin (photo above) learned new information about his daughter's murder a few months ago and now believes that Jamerson Mangrum is innocent

Hamlin's clock is running out because he's battling stage four cancer that has spread throughout his body, including his colon, lungs and liver.

'Typically, a stage-four cancer patient only lives five years. I will reach that point in October,' Hamlin shared with WGCL. 

Part of Hamlin's motivation to try and free Mangrum is the fact that he himself served nine years in prison. 

Dying wish: Joe Hamlin (photo above) met with prosecutors in Cherokee County in an effort to free Mangrum, but they denied to reopen the case 

Hamlin explained that he fondled his friend's 12-year-old son and police found hundreds of images of child pornography on his computer.

'It was a stupid mistake, one I regret to this day,' Hamlin stated. 

'I've been in prison. It's not pleasant. It is stressful. If he didn't do it, I don't want him sitting there.'

In an effort to free Mangrum, Hamlin met with prosecutors in Cherokee County, but they denied to reopen the case. 

'They don't want that black mark on their record that they convicted the wrong man,' Hamlin said.

Prison letters: Jamerson Mangrum (photo above) and Joe Hamlin have written each other a number of letters over the last few months where Mangrum has explained that he's innocent

His ex-wife, Donna Hamlin Tubbs told WGCL that she is disgusted by her ex-husband's efforts and thinks that Mangrum is her daughter's killer. 

'It's very sad that he would betray his own daughter and defend Jamerson Mangrum who is a convicted murderer,' Tubbs stated. 'He had his day in court.'
