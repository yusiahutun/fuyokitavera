Dancing on Ice: Ice made Chemmy Alcott stronger

The Brit smashed her leg to pieces during a race last year and feared she would never compete in the Games.

But now Chemmy, 29, left, says she’s going for gold in Russia in 2014. Chemmy explained: “It’s been the most amazing thing to come out of something so horrific.

“I couldn’t have got back on the slopes this year without the show. Mentally it has been brilliant for me, just getting back into the grind of training and learning something new.

“And you have to be so mentally strong for the skate-offs.

“My problems in my career as a ski racer was that I was mentally quite weak and I broke down a few times, especially when I had a good run and I had expectations.

“Dancing On Ice has made me realise I can be strong. I’ve never had the mental strength before but in this show, I’ve had to show it.

“It’s a massive turning point for me. I think my comeback could be special.

“The Olympics will be the end of my career and of course I want to leave with a gold medal.

“I’m the only person to come into Dancing On Ice to have less injuries at the end than at the beginning.

“I’ve always thought being girlie distracts from the sport, but I now know I was wrong.

“If you look good and feel confident then you are going to perform better.

“So if I’m at the start gate and feel like a glamorous champion then I think it will be easier for me to be that champion.”
