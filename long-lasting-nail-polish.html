The 11 Best Long-Lasting Nail Polishes of 2024

We’re overwhelmed, in the very best way, by the endless flood of nail-spiration from celebrities and content creators, and though every fruit-named trend is slightly different than the next, we’ve yet to find a single one that says a chipped finish is the next best thing. The best long-lasting nail polishes not only save working hands from denting their fine-tuned mani, but they also glide on smooth, spread easily, and dry fast. And, although we’ll never relinquish our nourishing cuticle oils and strengthening treatments, it’s a bonus if their formulas contain ingredients that boost nail health, too. 

Maybe one day the argument in favor of peeling, flakey polish will get legs (ripped tights had their moment after all), but, until then, we’re sticking with what we know works: A streak and smudge-free finish that lasts long enough to make it from our Monday morning conference calls to the Sunday brunch debrief. 

Best Overall

Essie Gel Couture Nail Polish

Amazon

What We Love: Even without a base coat this gel-like polish is extremely long-lasting and still easy to remove.

What We Don’t Love: Some shades require a couple of coats to see color payoff. 

Touting a stellar reputation among professionals and novices alike, Essie’s line of longwear polish is quick-drying, durable, and looks far more luxe than what its affordable price tag might suggest. We’ve even spotted its beigy-pink shades on Kim Kardashian and Jennifer Lopez. So, swoon with us over its artist’s wheel of pastel and deep-hued shimmer and glossy varnishes that swipe on easily — although the lighter colors may take a couple of coats — and cleanly with no globby trail left behind. Following its two-step system, the celebrity-manicurist-approved gel polish lasts up to two weeks, making it one of the safest ways to achieve the enviable, high-shine finish of a fresh gel mani without exposure to harmful UV light. 

Colors: 45 | System: Yes | Finish: Glossy | Wear: 14 days

Best Drugstore

Sally Hansen Miracle Gel

Amazon

What We Love: The gel polish doesn’t require a UV light. 

What We Don’t Love: It might clump with too many coats.

Don’t discount this drugstore standby — in fact, we’ve spotted the polish on Jennifer Coolidge and the Golden Globes red carpet.  It’s one of the best options we found when it comes to color payoff after only one coat, which is a good thing considering it could start to glob up after several layers. Plus, the gel-like formula gives you the same super glossy and long-lasting finish without harmful damage from a UV lamp

This polish comes in an impressive 54 colors, so there's an option for you whether you like pastels, vibrant shades, or a true black manicure.

Colors: 54 | System: Yes | Finish: Glossy | Wear: Eight days

Best Baby Pink

CND Vinylux Longwear Nail Polish

Amazon

What We Love: It dries quickly and lasts for seven or more days.

What We Don’t Love: It’s a two step system.

Shade Name: Candied

CND made a name for itself as a salon-favorite, and celebrity-worn (hi, Rihanna) polish brand because of its rich color payoff, ease of use, and luxe finish. Its palette of rosy hues is sweet as can be, and we think Candied is the ideal baby pink for completing any strawberry milk mani or for an everyday neutral. Thanks to this two-step, non-gel system — you need to use the top coat with it to reap all the benefits — your manicure will last seven (or more) days and keep its glossy look throughout that entire time. 

The polish immediately hardens, so you don’t have to worry about damaging your freshly painted nails as they dry. But even though it is a relatively tough formula, it also includes vitamin E, keratin, and jojoba oil to nourish your nail bed and promote growth, giving your nails a healthier look and feel over time.

Colors: 21  | System: Yes | Finish: Glossy | Wear: Seven days

Most Colorful Long-Lasting Nail Polish

Deborah Lippmann Gel Lab Pro

Amazon

What We Love: It includes beneficial ingredients such as evening primrose and biotin for nail health and growth.

What We Don’t Love: You will need a base and top coat.

Offered in a whopping 85 shades, Deborah Lippmann’s iconic gel lab nail polish features stunning brights, prismatic glitters, and flattering neutrals. Its pigmented formula shows up with just two swipes, yet can last up to two weeks with a base and top coat.

Best of all, it’s not just a polish; it’s enhanced with active ingredients, like biotin to stimulate keratin production and evening primrose, an omega-6 fatty acid that prevents moisture loss in the nail bed. Plus, the silk fibers included provide that long-lasting, ultra-glossy finish.

What to Keep in Mind

  • Color: To choose the most flattering nude nail polish, know your undertone: warm, cool, or neutral, then choose a light pink or beige with the coordinating undertone. “Another way to decide what color will look good on you is to look in your closet to see what colors you are already attracted to and look good on you – if you can wear it on your body you can wear it on your nails,” says celebrity nail artist Michelle Saunders. However, our experts agree that there are ultimately no rules when choosing your shade. “A non-negotiable for me is that the nail polish you wear has to make you feel happy and confident,” says licensed lacquer artist Sigourney Nuñez. “If it doesn’t, it’s not the right color for you!”
  • Finish: While your preferred finish is a matter of preference, whether that’s matte, cream, sheer, or metallic, some may last longer than others. For instance, “shimmer, metallic, and glitter finishes stay on a bit longer due to its added texture,” says Saunders.
  • Formula: “A main ingredient that provides longer wear is something called plasticizers,” says licensed manicurist Hannah Lee, who adds “This keeps the resin flexible once it's dry.” Some formulas also include nourishing ingredients to hydrate and strengthen your nails and the surrounding skin such as jojoba oil, argan oil, and biotin. And, for added peace of mind, you may want a polish labeled “10-free,” which means its formula omits 10 of the most common toxic ingredients, including formaldehyde, parabens, and sometimes fragrance, that can be hard on your nails and potentially your overall health. 

Your Questions, Answered

How do you get nail polish to stay on for a long time? 

Proper nail prep of the surrounding skin and nail plate is going to make a huge difference in the longevity of your DIY manicure, says Nuñez. After you’ve filed your nails, she suggests removing excess oils with a lint-free wipe and isopropyl alcohol to help with adhesion and create lastingness. 

Additionally, she adds that you should “Never skip a base coat – not only will this help prevent any nail stains, it also acts as a double-sided sticky tape and helps the nail polish color grip to the nail better.” All of our experts also agree that applying a top coat is a must as well, and Saunders adds “Sometimes I use two top coats for maximum shine and adhesion.”

Finally, never forget cuticle oil. It prevents premature chipping by making nails flexible and less brittle, “so nail products move with the nail instead of chipping off,” says Saunders, who often opts for 100 percent avocado oil. As Nuñez explains: “Think of it like chapstick for your lips or serum for your face – cuticle oil is a moisturizing product that can prevent your nails from getting brittle and breaking.” 

Why does nail polish chip after two days?

While chipping is a natural part of the wear and tear of a manicure, Lee says that “nail polish typically chips due to not including base and top coat, but other factors can come into play like how harsh you are with your hands, or if they are in water a lot.”

To help this from happening in the early days of your manicure, Nuñez suggests wrapping the free edge (the very tip of the nail that extends past your skin) with nail polish. “Swiping your brush at the free edge and sealing the color in will help with any early chipping,” says Nuñez, who adds, “You can also reapply your top coat on the third or fourth day to seal the color and have that maximum shine.”

How do you apply nail polish for beginners?

“Make sure to not apply coats that are too thick since it will flood the cuticle area,” says Lee.  And, pro-tip, Less says that “if you struggle with shaky hands, try and place your pinky down on something solid for stability.” But, since mistakes are inevitable, it’s a good idea to always keep a clean, secondary brush nearby. “If in the event you happen to paint outside the lines and get polish on the surrounding skin, dip a clean-up brush (it can be an old concealer brush) in nail polish remover to help get that beautiful salon-quality finish,” says Nuñez.
