Who is Diana King?

Where is Diana King from? Diana King is a singer and songwriter born on November 8, 1970, in the Old Capital, Spanish Town, Jamaica. She was raised in Jamaica by an Indian father and Jamaican mother.

Diana King ran away from home when she was 13 years old and ended up in Ocho Rios, Jamaica’s busiest tourist regions. Her experiences during this time led her to become the musician that she is today.

The talented songstress signed with Sony Music after making an appearance on the Notorious B.I.G. 1994 album Ready to Die.

Her first single, Shy Guy, from the album Tougher Than Love, was a massive hit, reaching number 13 on the Billboard Hot 100. The song was also certified gold by the RIAA in the United States of America.

Battle with multiple sclerosis

Most people are not aware that Diana King has been battling multiple sclerosis, a progressive, immune-mediated disorder. During her diagnosis, she was informed she might never walk again.

However, after months of a myriad of conflicting emotions, she chose to change by accepting her fate and moving on. She used her songwriting skills to reduce the stress and fear of realizing she was affected by the disease.

According to Diana King, she has never taken any medication for multiple sclerosis and only does mediation and listens to music. She doesn't feel her legs sometimes, but she is grateful for how far she has gone.
