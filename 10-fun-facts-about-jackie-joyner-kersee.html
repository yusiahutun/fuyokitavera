Jackie Joyner Kersee was born on March 3, 1962, in East St. Louis, Illinois.

Jackie Joyner Kersee, a legendary American track and field athlete, was born on March 3, 1962, in East St. Louis, Illinois. Her birthplace played a significant role in shaping her athletic career, as she grew up in a community known for producing numerous successful athletes.

She is a six-time Olympic medalist.

Jackie Joyner Kersee's remarkable athletic prowess led her to win six Olympic medals throughout her illustrious career. Her outstanding achievements on the track solidified her status as one of the most celebrated and accomplished athletes in Olympic history.

In 1988, Jackie Joyner Kersee astounded the world by setting a new world record in the heptathlon at the Olympic Games in Seoul, South Korea. Her exceptional performance and unwavering determination propelled her to secure her place in the annals of track and field history.
