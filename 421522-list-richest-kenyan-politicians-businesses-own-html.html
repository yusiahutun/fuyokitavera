Kenya is home to some of the richest politicians in Africa and the world at large.

The politicians own and control a huge chunk of the country's wealth, with a 2014 New World Wealth report showing that people with political connections own more than 50% of Kenya's wealth.

Kenyan MPs second-highest-paid globally

Even though Kenya is a developing country with a meagre GDP per capita of KSh 199,997.25 as of 2020, its members of parliament have been ranked the second-highest paid in the world behind their Nigerian counterparts.

The findings were revealed in a 2013 study by the United Kingdom-based Independent Parliamentary Standard Authority and the International Monetary Fund (IMF).

Besides earning KSh 710,000 monthly in salaries, the MPs goose lie in a stack of generous allowances, including a KSh 5 million car grant.

When allowances are added, their pay easily rises above KSh 1 million per month in a country where a third of the population lives below the poverty line.

Uhuru ranked fourth-highest-paid leader in Africa

In May 2021, Kenyan president, Uhuru Kenyatta, was ranked the fourth highest-paid leader in Africa with annual pay of KSh 20.9 million, Business Insider Africa reported.

TUKO.co.ke established some of the richest politicians in Kenya were handed wealth on a silver platter by their parents, who were also in leadership. At the same time, some attained it through their lucrative positions and businesses.

Others, however, have been put on the spot for acquiring wealth through corrupt means.

Richest Kenyan politicians

1. Uhuru Kenyatta

Uhuru Kenyatta is the fourth and current president of Kenya. He is the son of the country's founding father, Mzee Jomo Kenyatta.

Uhuru has engaged in active politics since 1997 when he first contested for the Gatundu South parliamentary seat in 1997 and lost to Moses Mwihia.

Besides politics, his sources of income are diversified with his family, where he is at the helm, having invested in nearly all sectors of the economy, including agriculture, finance, real estate and media.

Uhuru's net worth is estimated at US$650 million (KSh 71 billion).

In 2011, Forbes ranked him among the 40 wealthiest Africans.

2. Raila Odinga

Considered the enigma of Kenyan politics, Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga has a net worth of more than US$500 million (KSh 54.4 billion).

Raila generated his wealth from politics, business in oil and gas, land, manufacturing and other ventures.

He is best remembered for serving as the Kenyan prime minister from 2008 to 2013.

Before that, he represented Lang'ta residents as member of parliament (MP) from 1992 to 2007.

3. Gideon Moi

The Baringo senator is the youngest son of late Kenya's second president, Daniel arap Moi, and Lena Moi.

The KANU chairman who has set his eyes on the 2022 presidency has been in active politics since 2003 and was reported to be Moi's favourite son.

With investments in ranches, real estate and agriculture, the soft-spoken lawmaker has a net worth estimated at US$500 million (KSh 54.4 billion).

He has shares in Standard Group, one of the largest media houses in Kenya.

The Moi family where Gideon was chosen as the successor to his father also own huge tracts of land and has an interest in education, maritime and the freight sector.

4. William Ruto

Described by some pundits as a charismatic politician, the Kenyan deputy president is also an astute businessman.

The 54-year-old is a self-proclaimed chicken seller who rose to occupy the second-highest office in the land when he was elected alongside President Uhuru Kenyatta in 2013 before being re-elected in 2017.

The DP has a string of business ventures, properties, and investments contributing to his estimated US$400 million (KSh 44 billion) net worth.

He is the owner of the controversial Weston Hotel in Nairobi.

5. Mike Sonko

The flamboyant gold loving former Nairobi governor is among the richest politicians in Kenya.

Sonko doesn't shy away from flaunting his wealth to all and sundry.

Even though reports emerged he was into shady drug deals, he vehemently denied this claim, stating he was a clean businessman with investments in real estate, transport and other sectors.

"The only drugs which I know are the drugs that the doctors who I hire during my medical camps bring. Those drugs given by doctors and nurses are the only ones I know," he said at a past interview.

The 46-year-old's net worth is estimated at more than KSh 20 billion.

6. Kiraitu Murungi

The Meru governor, 69, has won in every election he has taken part in since 1992.

He is one of the politicians who fought for multi-party democracy in the country during the KANU regime.

7. Moses Wetang'ula

Bungoma senator Moses Wetang'ula began his political career in 1993 when he was nominated a member of parliament by the then ruling party - KANU.

He served as Sirisia MP for 10 years from 2003 to 2013. During his time as MP, he was appointed in various ministerial positions by Kenya's third president Mwai Kibaki with the peak being holding the Foreign Affairs docket.

Wetang'ula owns a posh villa in the Karen leafy suburbs in Nairobi.

His net worth is valued at more than KSh 10 billion.

8. Johnstone Muthama

The former Machakos senator has investments in agriculture, mining and real estate with wealth valued at over KSh 6.5 billion.

Muthama's Rockland Kenya Limited trades in precious stones such as gold, ruby and opal.

The tycoon also owns Nina Marie Ltd, a clothing venture.

He owns a multi-million palatial home in Mua Hills.

9. Mwai Kibaki

Some Kenyans have ranked Kibaki as Kenya's best president since independence. He completed his 10-year term in 2013.

Kibaki owns thousands of acres worth millions spread across the country. Some of these are Gingalily farm (1,200 acres along Nakuru-Solai Road); Bahati in Nakuru, 10,000 acres; Igwamiti in Laikipia, 10,000 acres; Rumuruti in Naivasha, 10,000 acres; and Ruare Ranch, 1,600 acres.

Besides having a luxurious house in Muthaiga, he has two others in Othaya and Mweiga, which look like mini statehouses.

His average pay per month in retirement benefits is KSh 2.86 million, which includes perks and pension for the former president.

He has a net worth of more than KSh 20 billion.

10. Amos Wako

Amos Wako, a member of the Senate representing Busia county, was Kenya's longest-serving attorney general from 1991 to 2011 when he retired and ventured into politics.

His main source of income is from law.

Wako's worth is valued at more than KSh 500 million.
