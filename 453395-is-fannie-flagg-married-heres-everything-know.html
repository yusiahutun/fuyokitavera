Fannie Flagg background

Fannie was born Patricia Neil on September 21, 1944, in Birmingham, Jefferson County, but she was raised in Irondale, Alabama. Her father, William H. Neal, was a businessman and movie theatre projectionist. Unfortunately, the details of her mother, Marion Leona LeGore Neal, are not public.

Fannie was an only child, and she studied at the University of Alabama. Her father encouraged her to read books and write from a tender age. However, she suffered undiagnosed dyslexia, a learning-based language disability, which affected her confidence as a writer because she had poor spelling and was a slow reader. However, she continued to nurture and pursue her passion.

At 11 years old, she wrote an essay called Why I Want to be Bald-Headed. The piece earned her the top place at the County Fair. Flagg started acting at 14 years when she joined the Birmingham theatre troupe for young adults and children, where she was a backstage crew member.
