Reading supporters' explosive cryptic message to EFL after they're docked more points

Reading fans have issued a brutal cryptic message to the EFL after the beleaguered club was hit with a fresh points deduction.

The Royals have been deducted a further two points, taking the total to six this season, over the club’s failure to pay off debts. The League One outfit’s owner Dai Yongge has also been fined £100,000 “for non-compliance” and urged to “adequately fund” the club or sell his shareholding.

The two-point deduction sees the Royals drop to 19th in the table, three points above the relegation zone. Fed-up supporters have launched a concerted campaign to pressure Dai into selling up.

Last month, around 1,000 Royals fans entered the pitch in the 16th minute of a match against Port Vale as part of a protest against Dai's ownership. Supporters are also furious with the EFL over their handling of the saga.

The campaign group, Sell Before We Dai, has blasted the league over the club’s latest points deduction. A statement said: “Given the ongoing sales talks of Reading, the timing of today’s punishment is spectacular. Imposing a points penalty which increases our chances of relegation and therefore decreases the prospect of our club being sold is completely misguided.

“The EFL claims to be on the side of the fans, instead – yet again – they disproportionately punish those who care most over those who care the least.”

The statement added that the “league cares more about the rulebook” than clubs like Reading and repeated calls for an independent football regulator to be installed. The group added that protests would continue, with a demo in London being planned.

After reading the statement, eagle-eyed supporters spotted the cryptic message within it, with the start of each line from paragraphs four to six spelling out “f*** the EFL”.

Social media users loved the hidden message. One quipped: “They aren't only good at Reading but writing too.” Another added: “That’s brilliant” while a third declared: “Outstanding.”

Reading acknowledged the points deduction and their owner’s fine in a statement and said: “The club can again confirm that Mr Dai is proactively trying to secure the sale of the club with negotiations continuing with various parties.”
