The best hotels in Loch Lomond

The Lodge on Loch Lomond

1

Cameron House

Courtesy of the hotel

Cameron House reopened at the end of 2021 after an extensive refurbishment. It has a perfect setting, right on the shores of the loch, with its own motor-yacht for guests to set sail in. Staff can also organise seaplane flights above the water.

The hotel has plenty to keep you entertained, including an indoor pool with a waterpark for kids, and several restaurants and bars. For the best views of Loch Lomond and the surrounding mountains, head to the Great Scots bar for pre-dinner drinks. In addition to the Boat House right beside the loch and the Cameron Grill, there’s impeccable fine-dining at Tamburrini & Wishart.

BOOK A STAY

2

The Lodge on Loch Lomond

Courtesy of the hotel

As the name suggests, this hotel is right on the shores of Loch Lomond, specifically on the beach at Luss, with panoramic views of the water. It’s just 30 minutes by car from the centre of Glasgow. The restaurant showcases the loch through its wall of windows.

Out on the water, guests can enjoy speedboat trips or yacht cruises, along with canoeing or wakeboarding. For something a little more relaxing, treatments by luxury brand Germaine de Capuccini are on offer at the spa. The picturesque village of Luss is within walking distance of the Lodge on Loch Lomond.

BOOK A STAY

3

Monachyle Mhor

Courtesy of the hotel

At the north end of the Trossachs National Park, Monachyle Mhor is actually on the shores of Loch Voil, but Loch Lomond can easily be explored from this base. The hotel has unusual accommodation options, such as a Fifties wagon and a reclaimed waiting room from an old ferry terminal, now a stylish cabin in the grounds with views of Balquhidder Glen.

Other highlights include its very own fish and chips van, a bakery and a tearoom.

BOOK A STAY

4

Mhor 84

Courtesy of the hotel

Mhor 84 is Monachyle Mhor’s laid-back little sister, more of a motel on the A84 route into the Highlands. It’s in the northern reaches of the Trossachs, around an hour’s drive north of Loch Lomond.

It’s a refuge for walkers, cyclists, runners and dog-walkers, and a perfect pitstop for when you’re in need of breakfast, a slice of cake or a wee dram. The motel is also where to head for a decent meal, with much of the food sourced from the farm at Monachyle Mhor, and baked goods delivered from its sister property’s bakery, too.

BOOK A STAY

5

Cardross Holiday Cottages

Set on the vast Cardross estate at the edge of both the Highlands and the Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park, Cardross Holiday Cottages are the perfect option for a self-catering stay in this part of Scotland.

The 4,500-acre estate, owned by Sir Archie and Lady Orr Ewing, spans parkland, woods and farmland. One cottage is surrounded by beautiful countryside in the hamlet of Dykehead; another has its own garden and a south-facing courtyard, plus views of the Fintry Hills, Campsie Fells and, to the west, Ben Lomond.

BOOK A STAY

6

Loch Lomond Luxury Lodges

Booking.com

Loch Lomond Luxury Lodges are near the village of Drymen within the Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park. Each lodge has one, two or three bedrooms, plus a kitchen, and guests can pick between a hot tub, sauna or spa bath.

The grounds include a play area for children and there are restaurants and pubs in Drymen should you prefer to let your kitchen gather dust. Activities on offer nearby include fishing, hiking, horse-riding and golf.

BOOK A STAY

7

The Inn on Loch Lomond

Booking.com

With the sort of bucolic backdrop Scotland is famous for, the Inn on Loch Lomond is an idyllic hotel, close to the water and surrounded by mountains. The stately inn has been offering refuge since 1814, with travellers from all over the world calling in for refreshments over the years. The fireplace is a cosy spot to warm up by after a ramble.

At the restaurant, diners can enjoy classic fish and chips or a steak from the grill, with traditional puddings to save space for. Of course, there’s a lengthy list of whiskies and real ales to try, too.

BOOK A STAY

8

Loch Lomond Waterfront Luxury Lodges

Booking.com

On the eastern shore of the water in the village of Balmaha, Loch Lomond Waterfront Luxury Lodges each have a hot tub, steam cabin, kitchen and views of the spectacular surrounding countryside.

The estate has a private beach, ready for activities such as sailing and swimming – and some of the best fishing on the loch can be found here as well. The wooded setting is 20 miles away from Glasgow; to Stirling, it’s 30 miles.

BOOK A STAY

9

The Loch Lomond Arms

Booking.com

The Loch Lomond Arms is easily reached from both Glasgow and Edinburgh, and it’s also the perfect base for exploring the start of the Highlands, as well as the famous loch. The 17th-century coaching inn is well versed in the art of hospitality, and it’s not just the views that are a tonic, though they’re a fine place to start.

Guests can buy fishing permits from the front desk, electric bicycles can be dropped off from a local rental company and four by fours are on hand for yet more adventure.

BOOK A STAY

10

Duck Bay Hotel & Restaurant

Booking.com

The star attraction of this Loch Lomond hotel is its restaurant, but there are rooms and cottages available for anyone who’s over-indulged. There’s a train station a couple of miles away, with services into Glasgow, or you can stay put and admire the views of the loch. You’ll also be able to spy Ben Lomond.

Duck Bay's restaurant has a terrace on the edge of the loch for when the weather’s agreeable. Delicious Scottish produce is served in dishes such as haggis bon bons with whisky sauce, Atlantic-prawn cocktail, neeps and tatties, and cullen skink.

BOOK A STAY

11

The Beach House

Booking.com

The Beach House has a quiet lakeside setting at Inverbeg. The boutique hotel is formed from eight bedrooms and is managed by the team over at sister property Lodge on Loch Lomond, which is three miles away. The two-storey house is on the banks of the loch – for the most magical views of the water, book a room with a freestanding bath tub by the window.

There’s a restaurant at the Lodge and guests of the Beach House will also be able to book in at the bigger hotel’s Amber Rose Spa.

BOOK NOW

12

West Highland Way Hotel

Booking.com

The West Highland Way Hotel is just outside of Glasgow on the namesake road, and a short drive away from Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park. As well as more traditional rooms with, yes, touches of tartan, the hotel offers bell tents for more of a glamping experience in the grounds.

There’s a car park on-site with charging stations for electric vehicles, along with a fire pit and picnic area so you can enjoy the great outdoors. Staff will be able to help arrange activities such as fishing, golf, walking tours and horse-riding.

BOOK NOW

13

Ardlui Hotel

Booking.com

The Ardlui Hotel on Loch Lomond is part of the wider Ardlui Holiday Park, which includes self-catering lodges and an area for watersports and leisure activities. At the hotel itself, there are 12 bedrooms, a restaurant with two bars and a view of the loch, plus an outdoor deck and a beer garden by the water.

Since it’s an official Whisky Ambassador hotel, you can be sure of being able to try several types of Scotch during your stay should you wish.

BOOK NOW

14

Queen of the Loch

Booking.com

Just off the A82, Queen of the Loch is a pub with rooms in the village of Balloch near Loch Lomond. There are 27 rooms, with comfortable king-size Hypnos beds and free parking, making it an ideal pitstop for visitors passing through on a road trip of Scotland. The pub is part of the Marston’s Inns collection, with cask ales from the company’s five breweries served alongside wine and soft drinks.

Dumbarton Castle is close by, or you can set off for a tour of the Auchentoshan distillery.

BOOK NOW

15

The Tarbet Hotel

Booking.com

The Tarbet Hotel is set in the namesake Argyll and Bute village, within the Loch Lomond and the Trossachs National Park, on the western shores of the famous lake. It’s on an isthmus with another loch on the other side – Loch Long can be reached via a short drive along the A83.

The lakeside hotel – which occupies an imposing baronial building, with turrets and spires – is a few minutes on foot from the village’s pier, from where you can set sail on boat trips around the loch.

BOOK NOW
