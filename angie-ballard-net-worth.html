Angie Ballard – The Athlete

In addition to her accomplishments in the business realm, Angie Ballard has made waves as an exceptional athlete. Angie’s determination and passion for athletics led her to excel in wheelchair racing. Over the years, she achieved remarkable feats, including winning the 100m female wheelchair title for open in 1998, 2001, 2002, 2004, and 2005, and securing second place in 2003, 2000, and 2011. In the 200m race, she achieved

gold in 1998, 1999, 2001, 2002, 2004, and 2010, as well as silver in 2005 and 2000, and bronze in 2006. Her prowess extended to the 400m, where she attained gold in 1999, 2000, 2001, 2005, 2008, and 2010, along with silver in 2004, and bronze in 2002 and 2006. Furthermore, in the 800m race, she secured gold in 1999 and 2001, and bronze in 2001, 2005, and 2006, while in the 1500 meters, she triumphed with gold in both 2005 and 2010.

Angie’s Resilience and Impact

Angie Ballard’s story is not just one of sporting triumphs and entrepreneurial success; it is also a testament to her resilience and impact as an ambassador for people with disabilities. Her diagnosis as a

paraplegic at a young age did not deter her from pursuing her dreams. Instead, she embraced wheelchair athletics and excelled on the international stage, representing Australia in four Paralympic Games from 2000 to 2012.

Her achievements have earned her numerous accolades and recognition, leading to appointments as an ambassador for various organizations related to disabilities, sport, health, and exercise. From advocating
