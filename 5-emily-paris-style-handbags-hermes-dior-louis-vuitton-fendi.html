5 Emily in Paris-style handbags by Herms, Dior, Louis Vuitton, Fendi and Bottega Veneta get no

Much of the chatter has been about the characterisation of life in Paris and the French in general – but the fashion choices have also been under the microscope. In charge of the wardrobe was Patricia Field, previously costume designer on the 2006 movie Devil Wears Prada and the era-defining TV series Sex and the City, which ran for six series between 1998 and 2004.

In every episode of Emily in Paris, the main character, played by Lily Collins, matches her outfits with eye-grabbing handbags. While her outfit choices are much debated, the handbag selections, including Chanel’s lambskin flap bag and the Round as Earth bag, as well as the Y/Project accordion bag, offer more sure and chic reference points.

Here, STYLE handpicks five of the latest and cutest bags so you can dress to impress in the way Emily tries to.

Fendi

For the new autumn collection, the Fendi baguette bag gets a floral motif in various shades of pink laid over the logoed body, with embroidered edges. Price upon request.

Hermès

The Sac à Malice bag has returned to the range, with a space travel theme featuring colourful stitched illustrations of rocket ships on the body and the planets of the solar system on the straps in suitably far-out style. Price upon request.

Louis Vuitton

The Petite Malle’s boxy silhouette is inspired by the classic travel trunks long used to transport valuables around the globe, with creative director Nicolas Ghesquière layering a nautical chain print over the classic monogram canvas, balanced by gold clasps. US$5,500.

Bottega Veneta

Complementing any outfit, the Fold handbag is a versatile, contemporary piece in padded intrecciato, highlighted by attention-grabbing gold medallions on the straps. Price upon request.

Dior

The iconic Dior Saddle bag strikes a modern glam note in blue tie-dye with gold embellishments. Plus it’s customisable with the perfectly fitting silver medallion embroidered strap. Price upon request.
