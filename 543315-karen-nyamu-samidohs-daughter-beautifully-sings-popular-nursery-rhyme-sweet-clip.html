Karen Nyamu, Samidoh's Daughter Beautifully Sings Popular Nursery Rhyme in Sweet Clip

  • Nominated senator Karen Nyamu shared a video of her daughter Teraya Wairimu, aka Nimu, beautifully singing the famous nursery rhyme Baby Shark
  • Nimu, who Nyamu shares with singer Samidoh Muchoki, clapped as she sang the song, changing the intonations for each character just as in the original
  • However, when her brother Sammy Junior tried to correct her, an unimpressed Nimu smacked him in defence, igniting several reactions from netizens

Nominated senator Karen Nyamu has wowed netizens with a lovely video of her daughter, whom she shares with musician Samidoh Muchoki.

Karen Nyamu: a proud mother

In the clip shared on Nyamu's Instagram, the young girl Teraya Wairimu Muchoki, aka Nimu, sang a popular nursery rhyme as her mother recorded her.

Dressed in a Tommy Hilfiger dress and white shoes, the cute girl beautifully captured the essence of the catchy tune.

As she sang, Teraya Wairimu changed her intonation to match the different characters in the song, dad, mum, and grandma, just as in the original.

"Baby shark, do do do do do," she joyfully sang, clapping along with the rhythm.

However, at one point, she mentioned the wrong character in the sequence, prompting her elder brother, Sammy Junior, to try and correct her.

However, the young girl was not keen on getting his help as she playfully smacked him, an action her mother said she cautioned her against doing afterwards.

The senator wrote:

"On this day, I didn't see this coming at all at all. You see, Tami Tami always bullied Nimu. Taking the remote from her, pushing her mpaka Nimu learnt to defend herself sometimes when unnecessary. Hapa nilikachuna sana kashike adabu though."
